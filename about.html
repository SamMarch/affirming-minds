My Story

Hi! I'm Meg, and I'm not your typical psychologist. I'm a proudly neurodivergent (Autistic and ADHD) member of the LGBTQIA+ community who discovered my neurotype later in life – an experience that fundamentally changed how I understand both myself and the field of psychology.

Like many late-diagnosed neurodivergent folks, I spent years wondering why things that seemed easy for others felt impossibly hard for me, and why I had to work so much harder just to appear "normal." My own journey through mental health challenges, late diagnosis, and learning to unmask has given me a deep, personal understanding of what my clients experience.

I bring both professional expertise and lived experience to my practice. Having worked extensively with Parkinson's SA, the Department of Veterans' Affairs, and in work rehabilitation settings, I've developed a special interest in supporting people through complex life challenges – whether that's chronic health conditions, neurodivergent discovery, gender and sexuality exploration, or navigating systems that weren't designed for minds like ours.

My Approach

I believe in doing psychology differently. Traditional approaches often try to "fix" or "normalize" people, but I'm more interested in helping you understand and work with your unique brain, not against it. My approach is:

Collaborative

You're the expert on your life. I'm here to walk alongside you, not to tell you what to do.

Neuro-Affirming

Your brain isn't broken. We'll work with your neurotype, not against it.

Trauma-Informed

Understanding how past experiences shape present challenges, without re-traumatizing.

Conversational

No power trips, no jargon. Just two humans having real conversations about real stuff.

Why Telehealth?

I chose to practice exclusively via telehealth because I understand the sensory and social challenges of traditional therapy settings. No fluorescent lights, no overwhelming waiting rooms, no need to maintain eye contact if that's uncomfortable for you. You can be in your safe space, with your comfort items, pets, or stim toys – whatever helps you feel most yourself.

Plus, telehealth means I can support people across Australia, bringing specialized neuro-affirming and LGBTQIA+ affirmative care to folks who might not have access to it locally.

Beyond the Therapy Room

When I'm not in sessions, you'll find me advocating for neurodivergent and LGBTQIA+ rights, providing training to other professionals on affirming practices, and constantly learning about how to better support the diverse humans I work with. I believe in walking the walk – living authentically as my neurodivergent, queer self while supporting others to do the same.
